Perturbation Theory in 1 Sentence

Perturbation theory is a method for solving a hard problem by starting with a related easier one and making small adjustemnts to get closer to the correct answer.

Perturbation Theory in Quantum Mechanics

In quantum mechanics (QM) we describe the dynamics of a physical system with the Hamiltonian $\hat{H}$. Example: The dynamics of a free (no external forces acting on it) particle are governed by its kinetic energy. We therefore describe it with $\hat{H}=\hat{E}_\text{kin}=\frac{\hat{p}^2}{2m}$.
We can use perturbation theory (PT) only when $$\hat{H}=\hat{H}_0+\lambda\hat{V},$$ where $\lambda\ll 1$, i.e. the perturbation $\hat{V}$ has only a small enough effect on the total dynamics.
